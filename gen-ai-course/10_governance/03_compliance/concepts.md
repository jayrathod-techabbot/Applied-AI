# AI Compliance - Concepts

## Regulatory Landscape

AI governance is evolving rapidly with new regulations emerging globally.

## Major Regulations

### 1. EU AI Act (2024)

**Risk-based classification:**

| Category | Examples | Requirements |
|----------|----------|--------------|
| Unacceptable | Social scoring, manipulation | Prohibited |
| High | Employment, credit, law enforcement | Strict requirements |
| Limited | Chatbots, deepfakes | Transparency |
| Minimal | Spam filters, recommender systems | None |

**Key requirements for high-risk AI:**
- Risk assessment
- Data governance
- Documentation
- Human oversight
- Accuracy & robustness

### 2. GDPR & AI

**Data protection principles:**
- Lawful basis for processing
- Purpose limitation
- Data minimization
- Accuracy
- Storage limitation
- Security

**AI-specific concerns:**
- Automated decision-making (Article 22)
- Profiling
- Data subject rights
- Data protection impact assessment (DPIA)

### 3. US Regulations

**Sector-specific:**
- Finance: CFPB, OCC guidelines
- Healthcare: HIPAA + FDA
- Employment: EEOC guidance

**State laws:**
- California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA)
- Proposed AI laws in multiple states

### 4. Industry Standards

| Standard | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| ISO/IEC 42001 | AI Management System |
| ISO/IEC 23894 | AI Guidance |
| NIST AI RMF | Risk Framework |
| IEEE AI Ethics | Ethically aligned design |

## Compliance Framework

### Steps to Compliance

```
1. Inventory AI Systems
   ↓
2. Classify by Risk
   ↓
3. Assess Requirements
   ↓
4. Implement Controls
   ↓
5. Document Compliance
   ↓
6. Monitor & Audit
```

### Key Documentation

- AI system inventory
- Risk assessments
- Data governance policies
- Model documentation
- Incident response plans
- Training records

## Data Protection

### Privacy by Design

1. Minimize data collection
2. Anonymize where possible
3. Secure storage and传输
4. Clear retention policies
5. User consent mechanisms

### Data Subject Rights

- Right to access
- Right to rectification
- Right to erasure
- Right to object
- Right to portability

## Penalties

### EU AI Act
- Up to €35M or 6% global revenue (unacceptable risk)
- Up to €15M or 3% revenue (high-risk violations)

### GDPR
- Up to €20M or 4% global revenue

## Best Practices

1. Maintain AI inventory
2. Conduct regular audits
3. Implement privacy by design
4. Document everything
5. Train teams on compliance
6. Engage legal experts
7. Monitor regulatory changes
8. Have incident response plans
